About #M4H

#M4H is a movement for Montanans by Montanans. The Affordable Care Act (ACA) continues to serve Montanans and Americans as legislation promoting equity and efficiency in US healthcare. Yet, Senator Steve Daines has attempted numerous times to repeal the ACA, even without a backup healthcare plan. Affordable healthcare is no longer a red-blue issue, rather, it is a humanity issue. The ACA has enabled rural hospitals to continue operating and provided everyday Montanans with access to quality healthcare. We must band together to express that Montanans are in favor of affordable healthcare.

The Health Care Affordability Act of 2021 (House Bill H.R.369) seeks to strengthen the Affordable Care Act by reducing premium costs of health insurances across all income levels. Its implementation would improve healthcare equity through appealing to a more applicable dimension of equality, while also strengthening an already efficient solution to healthcare.

Frequently Asked Questions

Learn more about the Affordable Care Act, the Health Care Affordability Act of 2021, and what they can do for you!

  • What is the Affordable Care Act?

    The ACA is designed to help the everyday American afford quality healthcare by achieving three goals:

    • Make affordable health insurance available to more people through subsidies (“premium tax credits”) that lower costs for households with incomes between 100% and 400% of the federal poverty level (FPL)
    • Expand the Medicaid program to cover all adults with income below 138% of the FPL
    • Support innovative medical care delivery methods designed to lower the costs of health care generally

  • What is the The Health Care Affordability Act of 2021?

    House Bill H.R.369, The Health Care Affordability Act of 2021, was introduced on January 19, 2021, by Congresswoman Lauren Underwood. The main purpose of this policy is to expand upon the ACA by making health care even more affordable by reducing premium costs of health insurances across all income levels. Additionally, House Bill H.R.369 would set a ceiling on consumer premium expenditures at 8.5 percent of income.

  • Why do we need The Health Care Affordability Act of 2021?

    The Health Care Affordability Act of 2021 is a crucial piece of legislation that would have immediate benefits in combatting the pandemic, and will also continue to produce the same aforementioned long-term benefits that have been enjoyed as a result of the Affordable Care Act.

  • The ACA and Health Care Affordability Act of 2021 seem like inefficient solutions. What else is out there?

    Between 2013 and 2016, the number of uninsured individuals in Montana declined from 165,000 to 83,000, a 49.7% decrease. Thus, the Affordable Care Act was efficient in getting individuals on some sort of healthcare plan. With that being said, average premiums have increased for most regions in Montana since the implementation of the ACA. However, one must also consider the fact that healthcare costs, as well as medication costs, have been increasing for the past few decades. The ACA was enacted in 2010, but from 2000-2010, the average annual family premium rose from $7,000 to $14,000. From 2010-2018, the same average annual family premium rose from $14,000 to $19,000. The Health Care Affordability Act of 2021 would strengthen a pre-existing piece of legislation that has proven to benefit Montanans by engaging a large proportion with healthcare. The financial implications of the ACA were not terrible; however, House Bill H.R.369 would make healthcare even cheaper
